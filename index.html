Join Our Community

Our Mission

We are a private group of homeschooling families with elementary students committed to co-creating opportunities for our children to learn, play, grow, and thrive through activities that nurture social skills, creativity, critical thinking, and a love for learning.

Group Guidelines

Member Participation Required

All members are encouraged to help facilitate events. Inactive members may be removed.

RSVP and Attendance Policy

Please respond to event invitations decisively. Notify the host directly if your RSVP changes. Register only for events you plan to attend. Repeated no-shows, cancellations, or tardiness may result in loss of priority for future limited-capacity events.

Invite All Members Only

When creating events, please invite all members of Saint Charles Homeschool Co-Op, not restricted to age limits and always coed. Do not open events up to the public, invite non-members, nor cross-promote without admin permission.

Hosts Cover Costs

It is the host's responsibility to pay for materials when leading classes. Hosts may ask for donations or collaborate with other members to share costs. Do not charge an attendance fee unless hiring a third party.

Be Courteous

Arrive on time, be kind, respectful, well-mannered, and refrain from gossiping or sharing confidential information. This goes for parents and children.

Focus on Homeschooling

Please keep discussion board posts relevant to all members and focused on homeschooling. Thanks for helping maintain a useful and beneficial space.

What to Expect

Ages 5–10

Events are geared toward kids ages 5-10. Younger and older siblings are welcome to tag along if necessary.

Experienced Instructors

All members contribute to hosting classes and events, sharing knowledge and passion for teaching to create engaging learning experiences.

Collaborative Spaces

We meet at library meeting rooms, residences, park pavilions, playgrounds, church facilities, gymnasiums, and hiking trails.

Diverse Classes

From core subjects like reading, math, science, and social studies to electives such as art, music, health, and physical education.

Community Events

Regular social events to help families connect, share experiences, get to know each other, and build lasting friendships.

Field Trips

Organized field trips and guest speakers for private presentations, providing hands-on experiences and broader perspectives.

Plus birthday parties, sports, games, camping trips, photoshoots, graduation celebrations, group presentations, learning expos, theater performances, Bible studies, and more!
